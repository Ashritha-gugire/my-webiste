Research Objective

Solving problems at the intersection of Data Analytics, Urban Mobility, and Behavioral Economics, using advanced techniques to optimize mobility systems, predict agent behaviors, and enhance urban planning for societal benefit. I use the following research methods:

  • Mechanism Design for Social Good
  • Machine Learning:
    • Supervised Learning Models - Linear & Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Jaccard Indexing: Applying these models to predict agent behavior, mobility patterns, and identify anomalies in urban environments.
    • Unsupervised Learning Algorithms:
      • K-Means Clustering: For grouping similar movement patterns
      • DBSCAN: Particularly good for spatial data as it can find clusters of arbitrary shapes
      • Hierarchical Clustering: For understanding nested relationships in mobility patterns
      • PCA: For dimensionality reduction of complex mobility features
    • Learning on Graphical Models & Causal Inference
      • Constructing knowledge graphs that represent agent interactions, urban environments, and mobility patterns to understand the relationships between various entities.
      • Using causal inference to understand the cause-and-effect relationships in urban mobility systems, identifying key factors that influence agent behavior and decision-making processes.
      • Building predictive models for traffic forecasting, movement prediction, and resource allocation, while considering the dynamic nature of real-world data.
